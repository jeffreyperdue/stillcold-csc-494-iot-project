- CSC494 should be the opportunity for you to evaluate yourself as a professional problem solver.

## How to grade your final CSC494

### 1000 points total

- 150 points from HW
- 250 points from midterm
- 300 points from Project
- 200 points from Learning with AI
- 100 points from evaluating peers

### Three evaluations from managers

- 300 points from each manager for the project
- 200 points from each manager for learning with AI

---

### Evaluation algorithm

Step 1: Add all the points (5 x 500)

- Self-evaluation (this rubric) 500 points (project + learning with AI)+ 100 (evaluating others)
- The evaluation results from three managers (500 points per manager evaluation rubric)
- Professor's evaluation (as the higher-level management, 500 points)

Step 2: Average the 5 evaluations
- For example, the total score is 2300, and the student earns 460 points
- Max 500 points

Step 3: The professor judges the evaluating peers

- The professor tracks each student's evaluation of other students (including HW3), and the professor subtracts points if the student is not honest (or not objective) or adds points if the student is too harsh
- Max 100 points

Step 3: Add HW + midterm + evaluating peers

- Up to 150 points from the assignment
- Up to 250 points from the midterm

Step 4: In the case of missing evaluating Peers

- The student will earn a C- or F (depending on the student's final results) due to the neglect of responsibilities.

## Q & A

Q. What if I don't like the final results?
A. The results will be from the average of your self-evaluation + others' evaluation, so I recommend you think about why some managers are not happy with your project results and/or learning with AIs. I can't share all the detailed evaluation results, but I'll share so that you understand why you earned the grade. If you still don't believe you deserve more, you should contact me as quickly as possible to discuss the issue and correct the final results (possibly) before it's too late.

Q. What's the Evaluating Peers about? How do you know if I monitored the three peers? What if I did nothing and gave myself 100 points?
A. I believe you are honest as a software engineer and problem solver, so I trust you. However, if any students haven't done much, it is easy to observe that because the students don't update any progress for themselves, both in Canvas and GitHub. Also, if I (or anyone) reads your evaluation of the peer review reports, it is evident that you did nothing or kept track of peers' progress. So, instead, be the manager (you'll be in the managing position sooner or later), and use this opportunity to simulate how to manage people. In the end, you feel anything is unfair, you can visit the professor to adress the issue.
